1

Validate Motion Accuracy

Do RTMM moves match instructions?
Steps
Initial Phantom Positioning

The CDR phantom head was placed on the platform. It was centered using the OBI and Varian couch. Its position was captured with AllignRT.

Introducing Displacements

Translational and rotational displacements were introduced using the CDR RTMM technology. They were performed for 3 translation and 3 rotations over a range of 3-30 mm and 0.5-5 degree.

Measuring Displacements

The displacements were measured using AllignRT. For each position, the displacement and measurement test was repeated three times.

Results

Average positioning residual error was 0.04 mm with a range of [-0.4, 0.3]mm / [-0.2/0.1]°

2

VALIDATE MOTION CORRECTION ACCURACY

Do RTMM moves match instructions based on CBCT data?
Steps
Initial Phantom Positioning

The CDR phantom head was placed on the platform. It was centered using the Varian couch.

Introducing Displacements

Translational and rotational displacements were introduced using the Varian couch.

Measuring Displacements

Measured using CBCT 3D-3D auto registration to acquire the shift values.

Correcting with RTMM Technology

Use of the CDR RTMM technology to correct the measured displacements.

Residual Displacements

Residual displacements measured using a second CBCT and getting the shift values from the 3D-3D auto-registration.

Results

A total of 74 residual displacements. Their standard deviations were 0.1mm and 0.04 °. These results show a very high accuracy with numbers comparable to the Varian’s PitchPerfect system

3

EVALUATE DOSIMETRIC IMPACT

How do RTMM corrections impact the dosimetry levels?
Steps
Initial Phantom Positioning

The CDR phantom head was placed on the platform. It was centered using the OBI and Varian couch. Its position was captured with AllignRT.

Introducing Displacements

Simultaneous translational and rotational displacements were introduced using the Varian couch

Measuring Displacements

The offsets were measured using align RT.

Correcting with RTMM Technology

Use of the CDR RTMM technology to correct the measured displacements.

Treatment Delivery

The dosimetry was evaluated using a single-iso, two target treatment plan to have a higher sensitivity for any residual rotational displacements, and with four couch positions. The displacement and correction process was repeated for each of the four couch kicks. The scintillator point doses were compared with the expected values from the treatment planning system, and the composite film dosimetry in RIT was evaluated against the expected dose profiles.

Results

The dose differences were under 3% for each of the arcs, and within 1.5% for the composite plan.
